# Schedule F and the Plan to Purge Civil Service

**ðŸ”¶ Policy Disagreement:** Supporters view this as legitimate civil service reform to make bureaucracy more accountable. Critics view it as enabling a purge of career professionals who aren't personally loyal to the president.

## Trump's Executive Order and Stated Plans

- **October 2020:** Signed Executive Order 13957 creating "Schedule F" classification [Source: Federal Register, October 21, 2020]
- Would have stripped job protections from **tens of thousands** of federal employees [Source: OPM analysis, Brookings Institution]
- Allowed firing career civil servants and replacing with political appointees [Source: Executive order text, legal analysis]
- Biden immediately revoked upon taking office (January 2021) [Source: Executive Order 14003, January 22, 2021]
- **Trump vowed to reinstate "immediately" if re-elected** [Source: Campaign statements, 2023-2024; Agenda47 video]
- Heritage Foundation's Project 2025 called for firing **50,000+ civil servants** [Source: Project 2025 document, pages 80-82]

## Critics' Concerns

- Would gut institutional knowledge and expertise across government [Source: Government accountability experts, NPR analysis]
- **Opinion:** Former officials called it **"the biggest threat to democracy"** in Trump's plans [Source: Former OPM officials quoted in Washington Post]
- Would enable removal of anyone not personally loyal to Trump [Source: Legal experts, Brookings analysis]

## Supporters' Argument

- Career bureaucrats ("deep state") resist elected officials' policy agendas
- President should have ability to implement the policies voters chose
- Current civil service protections shield underperformance and obstruction
